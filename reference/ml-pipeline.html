Model Comparison Cheat Sheet

Logistic Regression (Baseline)

When: Always start here
Pros: Interpretable, fast, hard to overfit
Cons: Assumes linear decision boundaries
Key params: C (regularization), multi_class='multinomial'

XGBoost (Primary)

When: After LR baseline is established
Pros: Handles non-linear patterns, feature importance, robust
Cons: Can overfit with too many trees, harder to interpret
Key params: max_depth (3-8), learning_rate (0.01-0.3), n_estimators (100-1000)

Poisson Regression (Goal Prediction)

When: When you want scoreline predictions, not just W/D/L
Pros: Principled for count data, gives scoreline probabilities
Cons: Assumes goals are independent (they're not, quite)
Key idea: Train 2 models (home goals, away goals), use scipy.stats.poisson to derive outcome probabilities

Neural Network (Deep Learning)

When: After simpler models are working
Pros: Captures complex non-linear patterns, portfolio showcase
Cons: Needs more data, harder to debug, overfits easily on small datasets
Key params: hidden dims, dropout (0.2-0.5), lr (0.001), batch size (32-128)

💡 Why >45% is actually good: Random guessing on 3 classes = 33%. Most matches between similarly ranked teams are close to 50/50. Research papers typically achieve 45-55% on international football. Betting markets (with massive resources) achieve ~52-55%.

Common Pitfalls

  1. Data leakage: Using future info in features (e.g., final tournament standings)
  2. Class imbalance: Draws are rarer (~25%) than wins — model may ignore them
  3. Overfitting to historical patterns: Football changes over time — 1990s data may mislead
  4. Uncalibrated probabilities: "70% win" should mean 70% of the time — test with calibration curve
  5. Ignoring the baseline: Always compare to "predict most common class" and to ELO-only model
